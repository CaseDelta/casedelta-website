## **Jason Fletes \- Family Law (02/02/2026)**

### **Current Practice & Pain Points**

**Document Analysis Needs**

* **Financial analysis is the most time-consuming task** in family law  
* Financial document analysis is critical priority over drafting (drafting is not nearly as time consuming)  
* Criminal defense is not doc-heavy, but **family law is extremely document-heavy**

**Storage & Organization**

* Currently storing **mass amounts of paper** \- major pain point  
* Wants to move to **paperless for economics**  
* Wants digital archive at end of case that is **easily searchable**

**Document Receipt from Opposing Counsel**

* Discovery usually comes via **Dropbox**  
* Format varies wildly depending on opposing counsel:  
  * Sometimes: one massive PDF  
  * Sometimes: many subfolders  
  * Consistency depends on "how nice they are"

### **Current Tech Stack**

* **Currently uses paper** (has looked at Clio in the past but hasn't adopted)  
* Previously used estate software (\~$180/month or $2000 one-time purchase with no updates)

### **Product Requirements & Priorities**

**Must-Have Features (Ranked)**

1. **Document analysis** \- THE most important feature  
2. **Financial spreadsheet generation** \- Auto-prepare Excel spreadsheet from core financials  
3. **Medical records processing** \- Record key diagnoses and information into document/Excel chart for itemized billed items during visit

**Lower Priority**

* Platform for document collection alone "does not command price"  
* Integration with other data platforms "not that big of a deal"

### **Security Requirements**

* **SECURITY emphasized multiple times** \- this is paramount  
* Security is "much more important" than integration features  
* Security concerns trump convenience features

### **Pricing Sensitivity & Business Model Preferences**

**Strongly dislikes subscription-based fees**

* Prefers one-time pricing model  
* Comfortable paying: **$100-$200/month**  
* **$500/month would NOT sell**  
* Reference point: Paid $2000 one-time for estate software (no updates) or $180/month

### **Time-Saving Philosophy**

* **Question raised**: "If you saved your clients time, would that reduce your billable hours?"  
* **Answer**: Historical precedent shows time-saving tools enable more cases  
  * **Zoom court example**: Reduced travel billing but enabled more cases  
  * **Westlaw example**: Faster case research vs. library visits \= more capacity  
  * **General principle**: Time-saving trends are good for practice growth
