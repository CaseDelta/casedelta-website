## Part 3: CaseDelta Origin Story & Product Evolution (~25:30 – ~36:48)

### The Pivot Sprint (2 Months)
Camren walked through his rapid iteration across four verticals after leaving Capital One:

1. **AI outbound cold calling for real estate brokerages** — Abandoned. Felt ethically wrong.
2. **AI inbound receptionist for dental offices** — Abandoned. Older patients hated talking to AI, and Camren didn't like it either.
3. **Med spa dormant client reactivation** — The pitch ("I can bring you back $50K in dormant clients") wasn't compelling enough. Nice-to-have, not must-have. Also faced gender dynamics challenges (98% female-owned industry made casual relationship-building harder for a young male founder).
4. **Law firms** — Landed here. Brandon Davies of Roth Davies was the first real champion.

Aaliyah's reaction: "All this in two months is crazy. I'm blown away right now."

### Brandon Davies as First Champion
- Cold-called from LA, Brandon (PI/criminal defense/family law, ~38 years old, tech-forward) said: "Yeah, dude, come meet with me."
- Brandon's openness: "I don't know what I need. You don't know what I need. Neither of us really know what we need."
- Brandon started referring Camren to other lawyers — "the firework" that kicked things off.

### Product Iteration #1: Document Verification
- AI tool to verify incoming client documents matched what lawyers requested (correct W-2s, bank statements, etc.).
- **Failed because it solved a paralegal problem, not a partner-level problem.**
- Aaliyah validated this: "A lot of legal tech companies try to solve problems for paralegals, which is fine. But hourly, it's harder to justify."

### Product Iteration #2: Secure AI Access
- Core insight: "I really just understood the fundamental truth that lawyers just wanna get their hands on AI, but can't do it securely."
- Ego hit: "They really just wanna use a ChatGPT wrapper." But Camren built it with self-hosted models and full data residency.

### Commercial Real Estate Detour
- Contract gig doing CRE lease auditing that contributed to CaseDelta's document processing architecture.

### Claude Code Market Shock
- Claude Code's release wiped ~$280B off legal tech market caps (LexisNexis, Thomson Reuters, Paxson dropped 15-18% in a day).
- Camren's existential moment: "Is this it? Do I go to medical? Do I go to med now?"
- Ultimately resolved that the disruption validated the opportunity rather than destroying it.

### KC Nightlife Aside
- Aaliyah recommended Black Dolphin as "the new Green Lady" (jazz club).
- Camren doesn't go out much anymore due to workload.

## Part 4: CaseDelta Product Deep Dive & Competitive Positioning (~36:48 – ~48:49)

### The OpenClaude / Claude Code Insight
- The real power isn't the AI itself — it's connecting AI across tools people already use (Notion, Calendar, Gmail, etc.).
- CaseDelta applies this "agentic coding agent" pattern to law: integrating with Clio, Google Calendar, Outlook, case management platforms, and proactively surfacing insights.

### "Seat at the Firm" Positioning
Camren's pitch crystallized:
> "CaseDelta is the seat at your firm that learns your institutional memory over time. There's no on-ramp. There's no HR. There's no benefits. But it learns your memory over time and doesn't walk out the door."

### ICP Refinement
- Actively moving away from family law and immigration (lower billing, more cost-conscious) toward higher-billing practice areas (PI, commercial litigation, employment, corporate).
- Aaliyah validated: "Family and criminal law are common entry points for early-career attorneys" — implying lower budgets and less ideal customers.

### Security as the Differentiator
- CaseDelta hosts all its own AI infrastructure using open-source models. PII never leaves the ecosystem.
- Aaliyah: "That's the biggest hurdle in legal tech. Trust. That's it."
- She validated self-hosting as uniquely important in legal: "In most cases it's not worth doing that, but in legal tech... that's really gonna be a differentiator."
- No other legal tech company Camren is aware of does this.

### Integration Strategy
- Clio = easy (open API). Camp Legal (immigration), MyCase, and others are secondary.
- Being deliberate about not building integrations nobody will use.
- Tech onboarding is fast if the platform has an open API. Sales cycle is the bottleneck.

### Competitive Positioning
- Harvey and Legora are enterprise-priced ($1,200+/seat, 25+ seat minimums) — they price out 80% of the market.
- CaseDelta owns the gap: 1-50 attorney boutique firms with zero AI solutions built for them.

- **Paymon** (Aramjoo Law Firm) — her aunt and uncle's firm, directly
- **Danielle** — connected to the Paymon/Amir network
- **Paymon as bridge to Mike DiPasquale's firm (DiPasquale Moore)** — Camren's direct outreach to the COO was left on read; Paymon could facilitate a warmer path

### Document Scale
