# Kansas City Lawyer Research Summary
**Research Completed:** February 27, 2026
**Total Lawyers Researched:** 123
**Focus:** Board leadership, fellows, and high-influence attorneys from Kansas City Metropolitan Bar Association

---

## Executive Summary

Comprehensive research completed on 123 Kansas City-area lawyers across two primary groups:
- **53 Board Leadership members** (current KCMBA leadership, section presidents, officers)
- **70 Fellows of the Bar** (established practitioners with significant bar association recognition)

This research provides detailed intelligence for CaseDelta's sales outreach targeting high-influence attorneys in family law, estate planning, and commercial litigation.

---

## Research Methodology

**Sources Used:**
1. Web search (Google) for attorney profiles, firm websites, news
2. Super Lawyers directory (superlawyers.com)
3. Bar association records and leadership databases
4. Law firm websites and professional profiles
5. LinkedIn profiles (where public)
6. Legal media and Missouri Lawyers Media

**Data Collected per Attorney:**
- Name, title, firm, email
- Bar leadership roles and positions
- Super Lawyers/Best Lawyers status
- Practice focus areas
- Years in practice
- Education and law school
- Thought leadership indicators
- Professional achievements and recognition

## Key Insights for CaseDelta Sales

1. **Multiple Entry Points:** Mix of large firm leadership (Russell Jones at Polsinelli, Brian Fries at Lathrop GPM) and solo/boutique practitioners (Janssen, Hansen, Daugherty). Different messaging for each.

2. **Specialization Diversity:** From criminal defense (Gyllenborg) to ADR (Daugherty) to family law (Janssen) to class actions (Fries). No single practice area dominates—customize pitches.

3. **Bar Association Influence as Proxy:** KCMBA Presidents and section leaders are decision-makers and opinion formers. Landing one can influence peers in same section.

4. **Generational Shift:** Mix of established (40+ year) practitioners and rising stars (Rising Super Lawyers, 2015+ presidencies). Next generation more tech-forward likely.

5. **Super Lawyers as Quality Signal:** 18+ confirmed, across multiple practice areas. Use as validation point in outreach ("Recognized Super Lawyers in [practice area]...").

6. **Women Leadership Gap Observation:** Noticed strong female representation in board (Vice President, Treasurer, Section Presidents), but fewer past presidents. Opportunity for DEI angle in messaging.

7. **Tech Adoption Readiness:** Lawyers with own firms (Janssen, Bratcher, Daugherty) or leadership roles likely more tech-forward. Boutique practices may value AI efficiency differently than BigLaw.
