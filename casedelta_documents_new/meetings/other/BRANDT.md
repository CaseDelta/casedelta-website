His preferred uses of Harvey: *"summarizing depositions, summarizing documents, coming up with a chronology — it saves scads of time."*

These came out of the conversation naturally — Perry describing pain that doesn't have a solution:

**#1 — Deposition prep (his most emphatic point):**
> *"If I'm taking somebody's deposition, spit out all the documents that they're on. Rather than I have to go hunt for them. That would be probably my main use — I want everything with this person's name on it."*
> *"And we don't really have that. We have it in that I can go search for it, but we don't have a vehicle for making it happen."*

**#2 — Cross-party communication filtering:**
> *"Let's say I've got a case where I've had two hundred communications with the other side — I only care about this one particular topic that may have eight. As it stands now, I have to go [hunt]. **#3 — Case synthesis from notes:**
> *"If you had something that could synthesize the notes on the client [from iManage]... that would be really nice. Because to do it now — let's say I've got a case where I've had two hundred communications — I have to go [through them manually]."*

**#4 — Chronological document analysis:**
> *"The key thing for me would be doing chronological analysis of the documents that exist in a case."*
> *"I would much rather feed a thousand documents in it and then come back with, 'Put them in chronological order and tell me what's key about each one of them.' And it can do that real fast."*

**#5 — Calendar / scheduling intelligence:**
> *"It would be really nice if I had somebody to do that."* (responding to Camren's framing of an AI associate that proactively manages your calendar)
