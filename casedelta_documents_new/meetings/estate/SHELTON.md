# **Discovery Call Insights: Bethany Shelton, Estate Planning Attorney**

## **Current Pain Points**

### **Failed Technology Implementation**

* Spent **10 months** trying to convert to **Leap** (which purchased Wealth Council)  
* Described experience as a "dumpster fire"  
* Major issues:  
  * Documents took **45 seconds to open** when auto-populated  
  * Documents wouldn't send over email  
  * Constant error messages preventing Word usage  
  * Extremely slow and buggy  
  * "Like using dial-up internet"  
* Recently extricated themselves from the Leap contract

### **Primary Unmet Needs**

**1\. Client Workflow Tracking**

* No current solution for tracking client progress through their process  
* Needs to track multiple touchpoints:  
  * Retention letter signed  
  * First half of payment received  
  * Worksheets submitted  
  * Drafts sent  
  * Review meeting scheduled  
  * Signing meeting scheduled  
  * Final payment received  
* Wants ability to see "where are they in the lifecycle" at a glance  
* Could be as simple as a checklist  
* **All tracking elements have equal weight** (documents, payments, scheduling)

**2\. Document Automation**

* Currently clients fill out digital questionnaires  
* Office staff **manually types** information into forms  
* Dream scenario: Client data auto-populates into multiple Word documents  
* Minimum viable: Office staff types into one form that auto-populates to all other forms  
* Key data points needed:  
  * Name, address, phone number, date of birth (for client)  
  * Name, address, phone number, date of birth (for key persons named in documents)  
  * Date of signing  
* **All final documents are Word documents**

## **Current Tech Stack**

* **Practice Management**: Recently left Leap/Wealth Council  
* **Accounting/Billing**: QuickBooks  
* **Scheduling**: Email (handled by office manager)  
* **Legal Research**: Law Why (AI tool from Wealth Council) \- described as "very, very helpful"  
* **Document Storage**: Appears to be file/folder based system

## **Rejected Solutions**

* **Clio**: "Really expensive" and has many features unnecessary for non-litigation practices  
  * Many broader features that "aren't super useful if you're not doing litigation"  
  * Know people who use it, but not a fit for estate planning focus

## **Budget Constraints**

* Leap was chosen because it cost the same as existing Wealth Council platform (no additional cost)  
* Haven't established a separate tech budget for new solutions  
* **Pricing threshold**: Would not be willing to try something for **more than $100-$200/month**  
* Price sensitivity is clear factor in decision-making

## **Client Workflow Details**

**Standard Estate Planning Process:**

1. Client signs retention letter  
2. Client pays 50% of fee upfront  
3. Client fills out worksheet  
4. Firm creates drafts  
5. Office manager schedules review meeting  
6. Office manager schedules signing meeting  
7. Review meeting conducted  
8. Signing meeting conducted  
9. Client pays remaining balance

**Documents involved:**

* Wills  
* Trusts  
* Powers of attorney  
* Instructions and related documents
