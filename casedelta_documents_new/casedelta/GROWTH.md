## V. Pricing Strategy: The Dual-Market Model

### The Problem with Pure Flat Pricing

Token costs are real. At $100-200/attorney/month in inference costs for heavy users (med mal firms processing thousands of pages), a 25-attorney firm at $1,499 flat generates ~$5,000 in token costs. That's a $3,500/month loss per customer. 84% of AI SaaS companies see 6%+ margin erosion from unmetered AI.

### The Problem with Pure Usage-Based Pricing

"How much will this cost me per month?" -> "It depends on usage" is a conversation killer with managing partners budgeting quarterly.

### The Solution: Dual-Market Architecture

**Two tracks, one product:**

#### Track 1: Self-Serve (Small Firms + Network Flywheel Fuel)

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Target** | Solo to 4 attorneys, any practice area |
| **Entry** | $50 free credits on signup, no credit card |
| **Pricing** | Usage-based: credits consumed per analysis/query |
| **Suggested rates** | ~$1-3/document analysis, ~$0.50/Q&A query, ~$5/case briefing |
| **Typical monthly** | $50-300/mo |
| **Sales motion** | Zero-touch PLG |
| **Revenue role** | ~20-25% of total revenue |
| **Network role** | Every self-serve firm contributes anonymized data to the Intelligence Network — this is the flywheel fuel |

Self-serve isn't charity. Every free and low-cost firm on the platform makes the paid product more valuable through network intelligence. The solo practitioner in rural Missouri contributing one case before Judge Williams enriches the data for every firm that draws that judge.

#### Track 2: Sales-Led (Target ICP)

| Tier | Monthly | Included usage | Target firm |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Practice** | $799/mo | ~500 analyses + 200 queries | 5-10 attorneys |
| **Firm** | $1,499/mo | ~1,200 analyses + 500 queries | 10-25 attorneys |
| **Enterprise** | $2,499/mo | ~2,500 analyses + 1,000 queries | 25-50 attorneys |
| **Overage** | $1.50/analysis, $0.75/query | Beyond cap | Heavy users |
| **Annual** | 2 months free | Same caps | All tiers |

**Sales motion:** Founder-led demo showing Delta learning their firm in real time -> 14-day trial -> close.

### Token Economics (Firm Tier Example)

```
Revenue:                $1,499/mo
Included usage:         1,200 analyses + 500 queries

Estimated token costs (Sonnet rates):
  Analyses:  ~66M tokens -> $270
  Queries:   ~8.5M tokens -> $37
  Briefings: ~$100
  Total COGS: ~$400-500/mo

Gross margin: ~67-73%
```

**Future cost optimization (don't do now):**
- Intelligent routing: Haiku for classification (80%), Sonnet for analysis (20%) -> 60-70% cost reduction
- Batch API for morning briefings -> 50% cheaper
- Caching learned patterns -> further reduction
- Inference costs fell 78% in 2025 — trend continues

### Pricing Testing Sequence

**Phase 1 (Customers 1-5): Learn, don't optimize.** Price whatever closes. Track token costs per firm, per attorney, per practice area. This data is worth more than the revenue.

**Phase 2 (Customers 5-15): Split test.** Half flat, half usage-based. Track: close rate, 60-day retention, usage volume, margin.

**Phase 3 (Customers 15-30): Converge.** Lock the winning model. Scale.

4. By demo day: 10-20 high-quality intelligence memories ready — judges profiled, local rules summarized, relevant legislation identified.

1. "Go to casedelta.com/vip. Type in your access code."
2. Lawyer selects their name, sets a password.

> "Welcome to CaseDelta. I've been preparing for your firm. Here's what I already know: 22 judges profiled in the Johnson County District Court. 10th Judicial District local rules reviewed. 7 new Kansas laws this year that may affect your employment law practice. This is just the beginning."

**Why this converts:** The lawyer doesn't have to imagine what Delta could do. They're looking at it. The intelligence is real, specific, and immediately useful. The demo IS the product. There's no gap between "let me show you" and "this is yours."
